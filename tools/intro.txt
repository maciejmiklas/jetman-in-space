 AFTER TEN CRASH LANDINGS, SIXTEEN

 QUESTIONABLE SNACKS, AND ONE

 ACCIDENTAL ELECTION TO BECOME

 MAYOR OF A ROCK,

 JETMAN FINALLY HAD ALL THE ROCKET

 PARTS, PLUS THREE EXTRA SCREWS

 THAT DEFINITELY DID NOT BELONG

 TO ANYTHING HE OWNED.

 HE STRAPPED IN, TIGHTENED A BOLT

 WITH PURE OPTIMISM, AND FIRED UP

 THE ENGINES. THEY MADE A SOUND LIKE

 A WALRUS PLAYING THE TROMBONE,

 WHICH, ACCORDING TO THE MANUAL,

 MEANS PROBABLY FINE.

 HE BLASTED OFF FROM PLANET NUMBER TEN,

 WAVING GOODBYE TO THE LOCALS, WHO

 THREW CONFETTI MADE OF RECYCLED

 USER MANUALS AND YELLED: VISIT AGAIN,

 BUT MAYBE DON'T LAND.

 THE STARS RUSHED BY, JETMAN SMILED.

 HOME WAS JUST A SHORT, SAFE, CALM

 CRUISE AWAY. THEN THE UNIVERSE SAID:

 LOL, NOPE.

 SHWIP! SHWAP! SHWONK!

 A METEOR SHOWER APPEARED, THE KIND

 THAT LOOKS LIKE GLITTER UNTIL IT

 STARTS REARRANGING YOUR PLANS.

 JETMAN DODGED LEFT, RIGHT, AND ALSO

 A DIRECTION PREVIOUSLY UNKNOWN TO

 PHYSICS. SPARKS FLEW. ALARMS BEEPED.

 SOMETHING IN THE BACK SAID MEOW.

 THE ROCKET DID A BARREL ROLL, THEN

 A PANIC ROLL, THEN KIND OF A

 WE'RE DOING THIS NOW? ROLL.

 KABONK!

 HE CRASH LANDED AGAIN ON A PLANET

 THAT LOOKED SUSPICIOUSLY FAMILIAR.

 BLUE SKIES, COMFY CLOUDS, A BILLBOARD

 FOR MAMA BLOOP'S PANCAKE NEBULA.

 JETMAN BLINKED, CHECKED HIS MAP,

 CHECKED THE BILLBOARD, CHECKED HIS

 HEART, WHICH WAS DOING JUMPING JACKS.

 THIS WASN'T JUST ANY PLANET.

 THIS WAS HOME!!!

 THE METEOR SHOWER HAD YEETED HIM

 STRAIGHT BACK TO PLANET BLOOP.

 LUCKIEST CRASH IN THE GALAXY.

 THE LANDING PAD ATTENDANT WAVED,

 THEN CHECKED A CLIPBOARD AND SAID,

 WE'VE GOT YOU DOWN FOR ARRIVALS,

 ZERO RESERVATIONS, AND ONE TAB AT

 THE RUSTY COMET.

 JETMAN STOOD, SWAYED, AND LAUGHED

 SO HARD HIS VISOR FOGGED. 
     
        HE'D DONE IT!

 HE'D TOURED THE GLORPUS GALAXY THE

 HARD WAY AND STILL MADE IT BACK BEFORE

 THE JUKEBOX FINISHED ROCKET MAN.

 HIS FRIENDS BURST OUT OF THE ORBIT INN,

 CHEERING, THROWING STREAMERS, AND

 DEFINITELY NOT LETTING HIM TOUCH

 ANY NAVIGATION EQUIPMENT EVER AGAIN.

 NEXT TIME, THEY SAID, WE CALL A

 RIDESHARE.

 JETMAN NODDED, PATTING HIS SINGED

 JETPACK AFFECTIONATELY. NEXT TIME,

 I'M TAKING THE BUS.

 AND SOMEWHERE FAR ABOVE, A METEOR

 MADE A LITTLE WINKING ARC ACROSS

 THE SKY LIKE THE UNIVERSE SAYING,

 SEE YOU SOON, BUDDY ;)
